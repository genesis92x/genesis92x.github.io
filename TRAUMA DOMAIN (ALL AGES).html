Sexual Abuse

This indicator describes whether the child/youth has experienced sexual abuse.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the caregiver or child/youth disclosed sexual abuse?
  • How often did the abuse occur?
  • Did the abuse result in physical injury?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of sexual abuse.
  • YES – Child/youth has experienced or is suspected to have experienced sexual abuse, including secondary exposure.

Physical Abuse

This indicator describes whether the child/youth has experienced physical abuse.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth been in a home where physical discipline was used?
  • Has the child/youth received bruises, marks, or injury from another person?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of physical abuse.
  • YES – Child/youth has experienced or is suspected to have experienced physical abuse.

Neglect

This indicator describes whether the child/youth has experienced neglect, including physical, medical, or educational neglect.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth consistently received adequate supervision?
  • Have their basic needs for food, shelter, and medical care been met?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of neglect.
  • YES – Child/youth has experienced or is suspected to have experienced neglect.

Emotional Abuse

This indicator describes whether the child/youth has experienced verbal or nonverbal emotional abuse or neglect.

Questions to Consider

  • Was there name-calling or shaming in the home?
  • Was caregiver affection conditional on behavior?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of emotional abuse.
  • YES – Child/youth has experienced or is suspected to have experienced emotional abuse or neglect.

Medical Trauma

This indicator describes whether the child/youth has experienced emotionally overwhelming medical events, such as surgeries, hospitalizations, or injuries.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth had significant medical procedures or hospital stays?
  • Did they find the experience emotionally overwhelming?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of medical trauma.
  • YES – Child/youth has experienced or is suspected to have experienced emotionally overwhelming medical events.

Natural or Manmade Disaster

This indicator describes exposure to disasters such as fires, earthquakes, car accidents, or bombings, either directly or second-hand.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth experienced or witnessed a disaster?
  • Was there ongoing impact or disruption due to the event?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of exposure to disaster.
  • YES – Child/youth has experienced or is suspected to have experienced a disaster directly or indirectly.

Family Violence

This indicator describes exposure to violence within the child/youth’s home or family.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth witnessed frequent or physical fighting in the home?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of witnessing family violence.
  • YES – Child/youth has witnessed or is suspected to have witnessed family violence.

Community/School Violence

This indicator describes exposure to violence in the community or school setting.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth lived in a violent neighborhood?
  • Have they witnessed or experienced violence at school?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of exposure to community or school violence.
  • YES – Child/youth has witnessed or experienced violence in the community or school.

Criminal Activity

This indicator describes the child/youth’s exposure to criminal activity, including witnessing or being victimized by crimes.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth or someone in their family been a victim of a crime?
  • Has the child/youth witnessed criminal activity?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of exposure to criminal activity.
  • YES – Child/youth has witnessed or been victimized by criminal activity.

War/Terrorism Affected

This indicator describes exposure to war, political violence, torture, or terrorism.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth or their family lived in a war-torn region?
  • Were they displaced or injured due to war or terrorism?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of exposure to war or terrorism.
  • YES – Child/youth or family has experienced or been affected by war, terrorism, or political violence.

Disruptions in Caregiving/Attachment Losses

This indicator documents major changes in caregivers or caregiving that may disrupt attachment.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth lived apart from their caregivers?
  • Have they experienced the death of a caregiver?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of disruptions in caregiving or attachment losses.
  • YES – Child/youth has experienced or is suspected to have experienced disruptions in caregiving or attachment losses.

Parental Criminal Behavior

This indicator describes the criminal behavior of biological or legal guardians (not foster parents).

Questions to Consider

  • Have the child/youth’s parents or guardians been involved in criminal activity?
  • Have they been incarcerated?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • NO – No evidence of parental criminal behavior.
  • YES – One or both parents/guardians have a history of criminal behavior resulting in conviction or incarceration.

Emotional and/or Physical Dysregulation

This indicator describes the child/youth’s difficulties with regulating emotions and physiological responses.

Questions to Consider

  • Are emotional reactions out of proportion to the situation?
  • Does the child/youth have extreme or unchecked emotional responses?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No difficulties with emotional or physiological regulation.
  • 1 – Some difficulties; may be jumpy, irritable, or have minor somatic complaints.
  • 2 – Problems interfere with functioning; may include mood shifts or persistent anxiety.
  • 3 – Severe dysregulation; may include emotional shutdown or intense outbursts.

Intrusions/Re-Experiencing

This indicator describes intrusive memories or reminders of traumatic events, including nightmares, flashbacks, and trauma-themed play.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth experience intrusive thoughts or memories?
  • Do these intrusions affect their functioning?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of intrusive symptoms.
  • 1 – Occasional intrusive thoughts or nightmares; no functional impact.
  • 2 – Intrusions interfere with functioning; may include trauma reenactment.
  • 3 – Debilitating intrusions; may include flashbacks or hallucinations.

Hyperarousal

This indicator includes symptoms such as sleep difficulties, irritability, concentration problems, hypervigilance, and exaggerated startle response.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child/youth jumpy, irritable, or easily startled?
  • Do they have stress-related physical symptoms?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of hyperarousal symptoms.
  • 1 – Mild symptoms; no interference with functioning.
  • 2 – Symptoms interfere with daily functioning.
  • 3 – Severe symptoms; overwhelming and disabling.

Traumatic Grief & Separation

This indicator describes the level of traumatic grief due to death or separation from significant individuals.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth experienced a significant loss?
  • How does the loss affect their functioning?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of traumatic grief or separation issues.
  • 1 – Expected grief response; history of symptoms.
  • 2 – Grief interferes with functioning in some areas.
  • 3 – Debilitating grief; affects multiple life areas.

Numbing

This indicator describes the child/youth’s reduced capacity to feel, experience, or express a range of emotions following trauma.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth experience a normal range of emotions?
  • Do they show flat or blunted emotional responses?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of numbing responses.
  • 1 – Some difficulty expressing or experiencing certain emotions.
  • 2 – Numbing impacts functioning; may feel detached or emotionally flat.
  • 3 – Severe numbing; diminished interest in activities, sense of foreshortened future.

Dissociation

This indicator includes symptoms such as daydreaming, spacing out, forgetfulness, detachment, and rapid personality changes often linked to trauma.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth enter dissociative states?
  • Do they become confused about who or where they are?
  • Have they been diagnosed with a dissociative disorder?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of dissociation.
  • 1 – History or mild symptoms (e.g., daydreaming, forgetfulness).
  • 2 – Dissociation interferes with functioning; may include amnesia or trance-like behavior.
  • 3 – Severe dissociation; memory loss, personality shifts, or confusion about identity.

Avoidance

This indicator describes efforts to avoid stimuli associated with traumatic experiences, including thoughts, feelings, people, or places.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth avoid trauma-related stimuli?
  • Do they avoid people, places, or activities that remind them of the trauma?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of avoidance symptoms.
  • 1 – One primary avoidant symptom (e.g., avoids thoughts or conversations).
  • 2 – Avoidance interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Debilitating avoidance; unable to recall trauma or avoids all reminders.
