Psychosis (Thought Disorder)

This indicator rates symptoms of psychiatric disorders, including hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, and bizarre behavior.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth exhibit unusual or difficult-to-understand behaviors?
  • Are these behaviors interfering with functioning?
  • Has the child/youth engaged in magical thinking?
  • Do they believe they have unrealistic powers or abilities?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of psychotic symptoms.
  • 1 – Some disruption in thought processes; may include history of hallucinations.
  • 2 – Disturbance in thought process impairs functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Dangerous hallucinations, delusions, or bizarre behavior present.

Impulsivity/Hyperactivity

This indicator includes impulse control issues and hyperactive behaviors that may pose safety risks.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child/youth unable to sit still or focus?
  • Do they act without thinking?
  • Do they report feeling compelled to act despite consequences?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of impulsivity or hyperactivity.
  • 1 – Some impulsivity or distractibility; may interrupt or push others.
  • 2 – Impulsivity interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Dangerous level of impulsivity; frequent, significant safety risks.

Depression

This indicator rates symptoms such as irritable or depressed mood, social withdrawal, sleep disturbances, weight/eating disturbances, and loss of motivation or pleasure in daily activities.

Questions to Consider

  • Is there concern about the child/youth related to possible depression or chronic low mood and irritability?
  • Has the child/youth withdrawn from normal activities?
  • Does the child/youth seem lonely or not interested in spending time with others?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of problems with depression.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of depression with minimal impact on functioning.
  • 2 – Clear evidence of depression interfering with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Disabling level of depression preventing functioning in any life domain.

Anxiety

This indicator rates symptoms characterized by fear and anxiety and related behavioral disturbances, including avoidance behaviors and panic attacks.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth have any problems with anxiety or fearfulness?
  • Is the child/youth avoiding normal activities out of fear?
  • Does the child/youth act frightened or afraid?
  • Has the child/youth experienced panic attacks?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of anxiety symptoms.
  • 1 – Some anxiety present but not significantly impairing functioning.
  • 2 – Anxiety interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Debilitating level of anxiety preventing functioning in any life domain.

Oppositional-Related Behavior

This indicator rates the child’s relationship with authority figures and their response to rules and directions.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth follow their caregivers’ rules?
  • Do teachers or other adults report noncompliance?
  • Does the child/youth argue or defy adult instructions?
  • Do they do things they’ve been explicitly told not to do?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of oppositional behaviors.
  • 1 – Some defiance present but not functionally impairing.
  • 2 – Oppositional behavior interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Dangerous level of oppositional behavior involving threats or harm.

Conduct-Related Behavior

This indicator rates the degree to which a child/youth engages in behavior consistent with Conduct Disorder.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth admit to lying when caught?
  • Has the child/youth shown violent or threatening behavior?
  • Has the child/youth tortured animals?
  • Does the child/youth disregard or lack empathy for others?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of serious violations of others or laws.
  • 1 – Some antisocial behavior (e.g., lying, stealing, manipulation) but not notably deviant.
  • 2 – Clear evidence of antisocial behavior likely meeting criteria for Conduct Disorder.
  • 3 – Severe aggressive or antisocial behavior placing others at significant risk.

Adjustment to Trauma

This indicator describes difficulties adjusting to a traumatic experience, with a link between trauma and behavior.

Questions to Consider

  • What was the child/youth’s trauma? How is it connected to current issues?
  • What are the child/youth’s coping skills?
  • Who is supporting the child/youth?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of trauma or well-adjusted to past trauma.
  • 1 – Some symptoms managed by caregivers; no intervention needed.
  • 2 – Adjustment problems interfere with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Debilitating trauma symptoms prevent functioning in any domain.

Attachment Difficulties

This indicator rates the child/youth’s ability to form healthy relationships and attachment patterns.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth struggle with separation from caregivers?
  • Do they attach to strangers indiscriminately?
  • Are their relationships marked by fear or avoidance?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Secure attachment; caregiver-child relationship is healthy and responsive.
  • 1 – Some insecurity or inconsistent responses; minor boundary issues.
  • 2 – Attachment problems interfere with functioning; caregiver misreads or avoids cues.
  • 3 – Unable to form attachments; indiscriminate or avoidant behavior; high risk.

Anger Control

This indicator captures the child/youth’s ability to manage anger and emotional responses to frustration.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the child/youth control their emotions?
  • Do they get upset or frustrated easily?
  • Do they overreact to criticism or rejection?
  • Do they have dramatic mood swings?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of anger control problems.
  • 1 – Occasional verbal aggression; others may avoid triggering outbursts.
  • 2 – Anger interferes with functioning; may involve physical aggression.
  • 3 – Dangerous temper; frequent physical fights; others fear the child/youth.

Substance Use

This indicator describes problems related to the use of alcohol, illegal drugs, prescription misuse, or inhalants by a child/youth. Tobacco and caffeine are excluded.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth used substances beyond experimental use?
  • Is there suspicion of a substance use problem?
  • Has the child/youth been in a recovery program?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No substance use difficulties.
  • 1 – Occasional interference with daily life; history of use without current problems.
  • 2 – Consistent interference with functioning; does not preclude unstructured functioning.
  • 3 – Dangerous or disabling use; may require detox or pose safety risks.

Autism Spectrum

This indicator describes the presence of Autism Spectrum Disorder and its impact on functioning.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth engage in repetitive actions?
  • Do these behaviors interfere with functioning?
  • Do they have difficulty with communication or relationships?
  • Do they self-soothe with unusual behaviors?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of ASD symptoms.
  • 1 – Symptoms below diagnostic threshold; minimal impact on functioning.
  • 2 – Meets DSM criteria; symptoms impair functioning in one or more areas.
  • 3 – Severe or disabling symptoms; high-end needs for treatment or management.
