Knowledge of Needs

This indicator is based on the youth’s knowledge of the specific strengths of the child or adult in their care and any needs experienced by the child or adult, and the youth’s ability to understand the rationale for the treatment or management of these problems.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the youth understand the needs of the child or adult in their care?
  • Does the youth have the necessary information to meet the needs of the child or the adult they are caring for?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Fully knowledgeable about the psychological strengths and needs of the person being cared for.
  • 1 – Generally knowledgeable but has mild deficits in understanding.
  • 2 – Significant deficits in understanding or relating to the needs or strengths of the person being cared for.
  • 3 – No understanding of the condition; unable to cope due to lack of understanding or acceptance.

Youth Supervision

This indicator rates the capacity of the youth to provide the level of monitoring needed by the child or adult in their care.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the youth set appropriate limits on the child?
  • Does the youth provide appropriate support to the person being cared for?
  • Does the youth think they need help with these issues?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Supervision and monitoring are appropriate and functioning well.
  • 1 – Supervision is generally adequate but inconsistent.
  • 2 – Supervision is very inconsistent or frequently absent.
  • 3 – Supervision is nearly always absent or inappropriate.

Youth Involvement with Care

This indicator rates the level of involvement and follow-through the youth has in the planning and provision of services for the person in their care.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the youth actively involved in helping to get services?
  • Is the youth willing to follow up on recommendations?
  • Is the youth uninterested or unwilling to be involved in care?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Actively involved and an effective advocate.
  • 1 – Consistently involved but not an active advocate.
  • 2 – Minimally involved; may visit but not involved in planning.
  • 3 – Uninvolved; may want the person out of the home or fails to visit.

Youth Organization

This indicator should be rated based on the youth’s ability to organize household, services, and related activities.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the youth need or want help managing their home?
  • Do they have difficulty with appointments or schedules?
  • Are they prepared for meetings or commitments?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Well organized and efficient.
  • 1 – Some difficulties with organization; occasionally forgetful.
  • 2 – Moderate difficulties organizing and maintaining household.
  • 3 – Unable to organize household; needs help.

Youth Marital/Partner Violence in the Home

This indicator describes the degree of difficulty or conflict in the youth’s relationship and the impact on parenting and childcare.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the youth and their partner manage conflict?
  • How is power and control handled in the relationship?
  • Does conflict escalate to verbal or physical aggression?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Functioning adequately; disagreements handled with mutual respect.
  • 1 – Occasional difficulties; generally minimized when dependents are present.
  • 2 – Frequent arguments; verbal aggression or property destruction witnessed by dependents.
  • 3 – Escalates to violence; physical aggression places dependents at risk.
