Severity of Use

This indicator rates the frequency and severity of the child/youth’s current substance use.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child/youth currently using substances? If so, how frequently?
  • Is there evidence of physical dependence on substances?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Currently abstinent and has maintained abstinence for at least six months.
  • 1 – Currently abstinent but only in the past 30 days or abstinent for more than 30 days in a high-risk environment.
  • 2 – Actively uses alcohol or drugs but not daily.
  • 3 – Uses alcohol and/or drugs on a daily basis.

Duration of Use

This indicator identifies the length of time that the child/youth has been using drugs or alcohol.

Question to Consider

  • How long has the child/youth been using drugs and/or alcohol?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Began use in the past year.
  • 1 – Using for at least one year with 30-day periods of abstinence.
  • 2 – Using for at least one year (but less than five years), not daily.
  • 3 – Daily use for more than a year or intermittent use for at least five years.

Stage of Recovery

This indicator identifies where the child/youth is in their recovery process.

Question to Consider

  • In relation to stopping substance use, at what stage of change is the child/youth?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – In maintenance stage of recovery; abstinent and avoids risk factors.
  • 1 – Actively trying to use treatment to remain abstinent.
  • 2 – In contemplation phase; recognizes a problem but not ready to act.
  • 3 – In denial about the existence of a substance use problem.

Peer Influences

This indicator identifies the impact that the child/youth’s social group has on their substance use.

Question to Consider

  • What role do the child/youth’s peers play in their alcohol and drug use?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Primary peer group does not engage in substance use.
  • 1 – Some peers do, but others do not.
  • 2 – Predominantly peers who use substances.
  • 3 – Member of a peer group that consistently uses substances.

Parental/Caregiver Influences

This indicator rates the caregiver’s use of substances and its impact on the child/youth.

Question to Consider

  • Do the caregiver(s) use substances? If so, does their use impact the child/youth?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of caregiver substance misuse.
  • 1 – One caregiver has a history of misuse but not in the past year.
  • 2 – One or both caregivers have been intoxicated in the child/youth’s presence.
  • 3 – One or both caregivers use substances with the child/youth.

Environmental Influences

This indicator rates the impact of the child/youth’s community environment on their substance use.

Question to Consider

  • Are there factors in the child/youth’s community that impact their alcohol and drug use?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No environmental exposure to substance use.
  • 1 – Suspicion of environmental exposure.
  • 2 – Clear exposure to substance use in the environment.
  • 3 – Environment encourages or enables substance use.
