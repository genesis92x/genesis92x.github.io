Impulsivity/Hyperactivity

This indicator rates problems with impulse control and impulsive behaviors, including motoric disruptions, often associated with ADHD or impulse-control disorders.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child unable to sit still for a typical length of time?
  • Can the child control their behavior in age-appropriate ways?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of impulsivity or hyperactivity.
  • 1 – Mild impulsivity; may yell out or push others occasionally.
  • 2 – Impulsivity interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Dangerous level of impulsivity; frequent, significant safety risks.

Depression

This indicator rates symptoms such as irritable or depressed mood, social withdrawal, sleep disturbances, and loss of interest or pleasure in daily activities.

Questions to Consider

  • Are caregivers concerned about low mood or irritability?
  • Has the child withdrawn from normal activities?
  • Does the child seem listless, sad, or socially withdrawn?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of depression.
  • 1 – Mild symptoms or brief episodes of sadness or irritability.
  • 2 – Depression interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Disabling level of depression; child withdraws from all activity.

Anxiety

This indicator rates symptoms of fear and anxiety, including avoidance behaviors and panic responses.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child show signs of fear or anxiety?
  • Is the child avoiding normal activities due to fear?
  • Can the child be soothed when anxious?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of anxiety symptoms.
  • 1 – Mild anxiety; child can be soothed and supported.
  • 2 – Anxiety interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Debilitating anxiety; child cannot function in any domain.

Oppositional-Related Behavior (36 months+)

This indicator rates defiance or resistance to authority figures in children aged 3 years and older.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child follow rules or directions?
  • Do they argue or do things they’ve been told not to do?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No oppositional behavior.
  • 1 – Mild defiance; not functionally impairing.
  • 2 – Defiance interferes with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Dangerous level of oppositional behavior; threat of harm to others.
  • NA – Child is younger than 36 months.

Aggressive Behaviors (24 months+)

This indicator rates violent or aggressive behavior with intent to cause harm. Applicable to children 24 months and older.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child tried to injure others or animals?
  • Do they hit, kick, bite, or throw objects to hurt others?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of aggressive behavior.
  • 1 – History or concern about aggression.
  • 2 – Aggression interferes with functioning; caregiver unable to redirect.
  • 3 – Dangerous aggression; caregivers unable to mediate behavior.
  • NA – Child is younger than 24 months.

Attachment Difficulties

This indicator rates the child’s ability to form secure relationships with caregivers and others.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child struggle with separation?
  • Do they attach indiscriminately to strangers?
  • Do they show fear or avoidance in relationships?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Secure attachment; caregiver-child relationship is healthy.
  • 1 – Some discomfort or anxiety; inconsistent bonding.
  • 2 – Ongoing separation issues; inappropriate boundaries.
  • 3 – Disabling attachment issues; may meet criteria for Reactive Attachment Disorder.

Adjustment to Trauma

This indicator describes difficulties adjusting to trauma, including behavioral and emotional responses.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child experienced trauma?
  • Do they have nightmares, flashbacks, or reenact trauma?
  • How do they cope with stress?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No trauma or well-adjusted to past trauma.
  • 1 – Some symptoms managed by caregivers; no intervention needed.
  • 2 – Trauma symptoms interfere with functioning in at least one domain.
  • 3 – Debilitating trauma symptoms; child cannot function in any domain.

Regulatory

This indicator refers to self-regulation abilities including sleep, feeding, transitions, and emotional control.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child struggle with transitions or sensory input?
  • Do they require more support than peers to regulate emotions?
  • Do they respond with aggression or tantrums?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Strong self-regulation; age-appropriate patterns.
  • 1 – Some concerns; caregiver adjustments are effective.
  • 2 – Regulation issues interfere with functioning; referral needed.
  • 3 – Multiple regulation issues; immediate intervention required.

Atypical Behaviors

This indicator describes ritualized or stereotyped behaviors that are unusual or difficult to understand, such as hand flapping, head banging, or repetitive verbalizations.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child exhibit unusual or repetitive behaviors?
  • Do these behaviors interfere with functioning?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of atypical behaviors.
  • 1 – Mild or occasional behaviors noticed by familiar individuals.
  • 2 – Noticed by unfamiliar people; interferes with functioning.
  • 3 – High frequency; disabling or dangerous behaviors.

Sleep (12 months+)

This indicator rates sleep patterns, including difficulties falling or staying asleep, nightmares, or excessive sleep.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child getting enough sleep?
  • What are the child’s nap and bedtime routines?
  • How does sleep affect the family?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Full night’s sleep each night.
  • 1 – Occasional disruptions (e.g., nightmares, bedwetting).
  • 2 – Frequent disruptions; seldom gets full night’s sleep.
  • 3 – Sleep deprivation; almost always difficult to sleep.
  • NA – Child is younger than 12 months.

Family Functioning

This indicator rates the child’s relationships with family members, including parents, siblings, and others in the household.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the child get along with family members?
  • Is the child particularly close to anyone?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No problems; strong family relationships.
  • 1 – Some problems or stress, but not major.
  • 2 – Problems interfere with functioning.
  • 3 – Debilitating problems; risk of harm or absence of positive relationships.

Early Education

This indicator rates the child’s experiences and functioning in educational settings such as daycare or preschool.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child have difficulties with learning, behavior, or social relationships?
  • Is the child enrolled in a special program?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No problems in educational environment.
  • 1 – Some history or evidence of problems; may be in a special program.
  • 2 – Difficulties with behavior, attendance, or progress.
  • 3 – Immediate risk of removal from program due to unmet needs.

Social and Emotional Functioning

This indicator rates the child’s ability to engage in age-appropriate social interactions and relationships.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the child get along with others?
  • Can the child engage with adults and peers appropriately?
  • Does the child show joy or sustained interaction?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Positive social relationships; no concerns.
  • 1 – Some problems; may need support to engage socially.
  • 2 – Problems interfere with functioning; may be aggressive or withdrawn.
  • 3 – Disruptions in social relationships; no meaningful interaction.

Developmental/Intellectual

This indicator describes the child’s development compared to milestones and the presence of developmental or intellectual disabilities.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child’s development age-appropriate?
  • Has the child been screened for developmental problems?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No developmental problems or delays.
  • 1 – Concerns about possible delay; mild deficits in functioning.
  • 2 – Mild to moderate delays; impacts functioning in one or more areas.
  • 3 – Severe to profound disability; marked deficits in adaptive functioning.

Medical/Physical

This indicator describes health problems or physical conditions that may impact the child’s development or functioning.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child generally healthy?
  • Do any medical issues interfere with the child’s life?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No medical or physical problems.
  • 1 – Mild or well-managed conditions (e.g., asthma, diabetes).
  • 2 – Serious conditions requiring ongoing treatment.
  • 3 – Life-threatening condition; immediate action needed.

Self-Harm (12 months+)

This indicator rates behaviors such as head-banging or self-biting that could result in physical harm.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child engaged in self-harming behaviors?
  • Can caregivers stop the behavior?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of self-harm.
  • 1 – Some evidence; controllable by caregiver.
  • 2 – Interferes with functioning; not controllable by caregiver.
  • 3 – Dangerous behavior; puts child at risk.
  • NA – Child is younger than 12 months.

Exploited

This indicator describes a history or risk of the child being the object of abuse or exploitation, including sexual or psychological abuse.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child ever been victimized?
  • Is the child currently at risk of being taken advantage of?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No history or risk of exploitation.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of past exploitation; no current risk.
  • 2 – Recently exploited; not at acute risk.
  • 3 – Recently exploited and at acute risk of re-exploitation.

Prenatal Care

This indicator refers to the health care and pregnancy-related illness of the mother that impacted the child in utero.

Questions to Consider

  • What kind of prenatal care did the biological mother receive?
  • Were there any unusual illnesses or risks during pregnancy?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Adequate prenatal care; no pregnancy-related illness.
  • 1 – Some shortcomings in care or mild illness.
  • 2 – Poor care or moderate illness; care began late in pregnancy.
  • 3 – No prenatal care or severe illness (e.g., toxemia).

Exposure

This indicator describes the child’s exposure to environmental toxins or substance use before or after birth.

Questions to Consider

  • Was the child exposed to substances or toxins during or after pregnancy?
  • Is there current exposure in the home or community?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No exposure to toxins or substances.
  • 1 – Some exposure (e.g., tobacco, lead); no current symptoms.
  • 2 – Significant in utero exposure or current exposure to toxins.
  • 3 – Exposure with symptoms or ongoing risk (e.g., withdrawal at birth).

Labor and Delivery

This indicator refers to complications during childbirth that may affect the child’s development.

Questions to Consider

  • Were there any unusual circumstances during labor and delivery?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Normal labor and delivery; Apgar score 7–10.
  • 1 – Mild problems (e.g., emergency C-section); no lasting impact.
  • 2 – Problems with temporary functional difficulties (e.g., fetal distress).
  • 3 – Severe complications with long-term implications (e.g., brain damage).

Birth Weight

This indicator describes the child’s birth weight compared to typical developmental norms.

Questions to Consider

  • How did the child’s birth weight compare to typical averages?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Normal birth weight (≥ 2500 grams).
  • 1 – Underweight (1500–2499 grams).
  • 2 – Very low birth weight (1000–1499 grams); developmental risk present.
  • 3 – Extremely low birth weight (< 1000 grams); life-threatening risk.

Failure to Thrive

This indicator rates problems with weight gain or growth, including past or current concerns.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child had problems with weight gain or growth?
  • Are there concerns about eating habits or medical growth charts?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of failure to thrive.
  • 1 – Past or mild concerns; currently stable or improving.
  • 2 – Active growth or weight issues; below 5th percentile or significant drop in growth curve.
  • 3 – Serious medical risk due to weight or growth issues.

Family Strengths

This indicator refers to the presence of family identity, love, and communication among family members.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the child get along with family members?
  • Is there a strong, supportive relationship with at least one family member?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Strong, loving relationships; fully included in family activities.
  • 1 – Some good relationships; limited support available.
  • 2 – Relationships exist but are not supportive or functional.
  • 3 – No identified family or supportive relationships.

Interpersonal

This indicator identifies the child’s ability to form and maintain social relationships.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the child interact with others?
  • Do they initiate or respond to social interactions?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Strong interpersonal skills; initiates and maintains relationships.
  • 1 – Responds positively to others but may not initiate interactions.
  • 2 – Limited social interest or responsiveness; shy or withdrawn.
  • 3 – No age-appropriate social gestures; avoids or ignores others.

Natural Supports

This indicator refers to unpaid helpers in the child’s natural environment who provide social support. Family members and paid caregivers are excluded.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child go to others for comfort and support outside of family?
  • Are there non-family members who are positive influences?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Significant natural supports actively contribute to development.
  • 1 – Some natural supports provide occasional assistance.
  • 2 – Supports identified but not actively contributing to development.
  • 3 – No known natural supports outside of family or paid caregivers.

Resiliency (Persistence and Adaptability)

This indicator refers to how the child reacts to new situations, transitions, and challenges, including their ability to persist through difficulty.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child persist through frustration?
  • Can they transition between activities easily?
  • Do they need adult support to adapt to changes?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Strong adaptability and persistence; centerpiece strength.
  • 1 – Good curiosity and some persistence; needs further development.
  • 2 – Some ability to persist; needs caregiver support.
  • 3 – Difficulty coping with challenges; development at risk.

Relationship Permanence

This indicator refers to the stability and consistency of significant relationships in the child’s life.

Questions to Consider

  • Has anyone been consistently present in the child’s life?
  • Are there stable adult relationships?
  • Has the child experienced multiple placements?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Very stable relationships; likely to remain stable.
  • 1 – Stable relationships exist but may be at risk of change.
  • 2 – At least one stable relationship, but history of instability.
  • 3 – No stability in relationships.

Playfulness

This indicator rates the degree to which a child is given opportunities for and participates in age-appropriate play.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the child easily engaged in play?
  • Can they initiate and sustain play?
  • Do they need adult support to play?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Consistently demonstrates developmentally appropriate, spontaneous, and enjoyable play.
  • 1 – Demonstrates appropriate play most of the time; needs some support.
  • 2 – Enjoys play with support; lacks investment or enjoyment.
  • 3 – Does not demonstrate developmentally appropriate or quality play.
