Frequency of Running

This indicator describes how often the child/youth runs away.

Question to Consider

  • How often does the child/youth run away?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Has only run once in the past year.
  • 1 – Has run on multiple occasions in the past year.
  • 2 – Runs often but not always.
  • 3 – Runs at every opportunity.

Consistency of Destination

This indicator describes whether the child/youth runs away to the same place, area, or neighborhood.

Question to Consider

  • Does the child/youth always run to the same spot?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Always runs to the same location.
  • 1 – Generally runs to the same location or neighborhood.
  • 2 – Runs to the same community, but specific locations change.
  • 3 – Runs to no planned destination.

Safety of Destination

This indicator describes how safe the area is where the child/youth runs to.

Question to Consider

  • Does the child/youth run to safe locations?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Runs to a safe environment that meets basic needs (e.g., food, shelter).
  • 1 – Runs to generally safe environments, though they may be unstable or variable.
  • 2 – Runs to generally unsafe environments that cannot meet basic needs.
  • 3 – Runs to very unsafe environments where the likelihood of victimization is high.

Likelihood of Return on Own

This indicator describes whether the child/youth returns from a running episode on their own, whether they need prompting, or whether they need to be brought back by force (e.g., police).

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth usually return home on their own?
  • Is adult/external intervention needed?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Will return from run on their own without prompting.
  • 1 – Will return when found, but not without being found.
  • 2 – Makes themselves difficult to find and/or passively resists return.
  • 3 – Makes repeated efforts to hide and/or actively resists return.
