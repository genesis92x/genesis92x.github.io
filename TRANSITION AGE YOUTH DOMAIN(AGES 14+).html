Interpersonal Problems

This indicator identifies problems with functioning and behavior due to rigid and maladaptive patterns of perceiving and relating to others, consistent with personality disorders.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the youth exhibit inflexible or maladaptive traits?
  • Do they have difficulty relating to others or show signs of social isolation?
  • Has the youth been diagnosed with a personality disorder?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of interpersonal problems.
  • 1 – Mild interpersonal challenges or suspicion of personality disorder.
  • 2 – Relationship problems interfere with functioning; may warrant diagnosis.
  • 3 – Severe interpersonal problems; disabling and block independent functioning.

Independent Living Skills

This indicator describes the youth’s ability to manage daily tasks and responsibilities necessary for independent living.

Questions to Consider

  • Can the youth manage money, cook, clean, and maintain a schedule?
  • Have they lived independently before?
  • What skills still need development?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Fully capable of independent living.
  • 1 – Mild impairment; needs training or supervision.
  • 2 – Moderate impairment; needs in-home services or supports.
  • 3 – Profound impairment; requires structured living environment.

Parenting/Caregiving Roles

This indicator rates the youth’s functioning in parenting or caregiving roles, including pregnancy or caring for a dependent adult.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the youth a parent or caregiver?
  • Are they able to meet caregiving responsibilities?
  • Do these responsibilities interfere with other life domains?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No caregiving role or functioning well in that role.
  • 1 – Occasional difficulties with caregiving responsibilities.
  • 2 – Struggles with caregiving; interferes with functioning.
  • 3 – Unable to meet caregiving responsibilities; disabling impact.

Job Functioning (Ages 16+)

This indicator describes the youth’s functioning in a job setting, including attendance, performance, and relationships at work.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the youth able to meet expectations at work?
  • Do they have regular conflicts or performance issues?
  • Are they timely and responsible?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Excelling in a job environment; no problems.
  • 1 – Minor issues or history of problems; functioning adequately.
  • 2 – Some problems with attendance or performance; may need support.
  • 3 – Serious problems; recently fired or at high risk of termination.
  • NA – Youth is under 16 or not currently working.

Medication Adherence

This indicator focuses on the youth’s willingness and participation in taking prescribed medications.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the youth prescribed medication?
  • Do they take it as prescribed?
  • Have they ever refused or misused medication?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Fully adherent or not on medication.
  • 1 – Occasionally needs reminders; history of noncompliance.
  • 2 – Somewhat non-compliant; inconsistent adherence.
  • 3 – Refuses or dangerously misuses medication.

Intimate Relationships

This indicator rates the youth’s current status in romantic or intimate relationships.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the youth in a healthy relationship?
  • Is the relationship supportive or problematic?
  • Is there any history of domestic violence or dependency?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Strong, positive relationship or no current relationship.
  • 1 – Generally positive relationship; may impede development at times.
  • 2 – History of unhealthy or dependent relationships.
  • 3 – Currently in a negative or violent relationship.

Transportation

This indicator rates the youth’s access to transportation needed for treatment and life activities.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the youth have reliable transportation?
  • Do they require a special vehicle?
  • Can they access public transportation?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No unmet transportation needs.
  • 1 – Occasional needs; manageable with minimal support.
  • 2 – Frequent needs or requires special vehicle; access is difficult.
  • 3 – Completely reliant on others; frequent needs with no access.

Educational Attainment

This indicator rates the youth’s progress toward their educational goals and how it impacts their vocational functioning.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the youth have educational goals?
  • Are they making progress?
  • Does their level of education impact employment opportunities?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Goals achieved or no goals with no impact on functioning.
  • 1 – Goals set and progress being made.
  • 2 – Goals set but no progress.
  • 3 – No goals; lack of education interferes with functioning.
