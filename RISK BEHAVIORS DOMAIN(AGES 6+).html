Suicide Risk

This indicator describes the presence of thoughts or behaviors aimed at taking one’s life, including suicidal ideation and significant self-injurious behavior.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth ever talked about a wish or plan to die?
  • Has the child/youth ever attempted suicide?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of suicidal ideation.
  • 1 – History of ideation or behavior, but none recently.
  • 2 – Recent ideation or gesture; not acute.
  • 3 – Current ideation with intent or command hallucinations involving self-harm.

Non-Suicidal Self-Injurious Behavior

This indicator includes repetitive, physically harmful behavior that serves as a self-soothing function (e.g., cutting, burning, head banging).

Questions to Consider

  • Does the child/youth purposely hurt themselves?
  • Does the behavior serve to numb or redirect emotional pain?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of self-injury.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of self-injury.
  • 2 – Engaged in self-injury not requiring medical attention.
  • 3 – Self-injury requiring medical intervention; health at risk.

Other Self-Harm (Recklessness)

This indicator includes reckless behaviors that place the child/youth or others in danger, excluding suicidal or self-injurious behaviors.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth acted in ways that might be dangerous to themselves?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of reckless behavior.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of mild reckless behavior.
  • 2 – Engaged in reckless behavior placing them in danger.
  • 3 – Behavior places them at immediate risk of death.

Danger to Others

This indicator rates violent or aggressive behavior intended to cause significant bodily harm to others.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth injured others on purpose?
  • Do they get into physical fights or make threats?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of aggression or threats.
  • 1 – History of aggression or threats.
  • 2 – Occasional aggression or verbal threats.
  • 3 – Acute homicidal ideation or frequent, dangerous aggression.

Sexual Aggression

This indicator describes aggressive sexual behavior or behavior where the child/youth takes advantage of a younger or less powerful individual.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth been accused of sexual aggression or harassment?
  • Have they had inappropriate sexual contact with a younger child?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of sexually aggressive behavior.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of inappropriate behavior (e.g., harassing talk).
  • 2 – Behavior negatively impacts functioning (e.g., inappropriate touching).
  • 3 – Dangerous level of behavior (e.g., rape or sexual abuse involving penetration).

Runaway

This indicator describes the risk of running away or actual runaway behavior.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth ever run away?
  • Where did they go, and how long were they gone?
  • Do they threaten to run away?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No history or ideation of running away.
  • 1 – History or ideation of running away; no recent incidents.
  • 2 – Has run away once or from one setting.
  • 3 – Recent runaway behavior; imminent flight risk.

Delinquent Behavior

This indicator includes delinquent behavior and status offenses such as truancy, curfew violations, vandalism, or underage substance use.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth broken any laws?
  • Have they been arrested?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence or history of delinquent behavior.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of delinquency; no recent incidents.
  • 2 – Currently engaged in delinquent behavior (e.g., shoplifting).
  • 3 – Serious recent acts (e.g., car theft, gang involvement).

Fire Setting

This indicator refers to intentional fire setting that may be dangerous to the child/youth or others.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the child/youth ever started a fire?
  • Did the fire pose a risk to anyone?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No evidence of fire setting.
  • 1 – History of fire setting; not recent.
  • 2 – Recent fire setting; not life-threatening.
  • 3 – Fire setting endangered lives (e.g., attempted arson).
