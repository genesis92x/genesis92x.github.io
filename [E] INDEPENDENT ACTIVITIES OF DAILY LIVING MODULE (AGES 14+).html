Meal Preparation

This indicator describes youth’s ability to prepare healthy meals for themselves.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the youth able to prepare their own meals?
  • Are they able to use kitchen appliances appropriately to prepare their meals?
  • Is the youth able to prepare meals safely and make good food choices?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Fully independent in preparing meals; selects and safely prepares healthy food.
  • 1 – Generally prepares meals independently but makes poor choices or relies on fast food.
  • 2 – Struggles with safe meal preparation; can prepare basic foods but not cook.
  • 3 – Not currently able to safely prepare meals or select appropriate portions.

Shopping

This indicator describes youth’s ability to budget, select items, or plan for multiple shopping needs.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the youth shop independently for themselves?
  • Are they able to plan, budget and make good choices regarding their shopping priorities?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Can shop independently to meet all needs.
  • 1 – Can shop independently but may struggle with spending or item selection.
  • 2 – Struggles with shopping; challenges interfere with functioning.
  • 3 – Unable to shop to meet basic needs; choices pose significant risk.

Housework

This indicator describes youth’s ability to keep a functioning and clean living space independently.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the youth able to keep their living space clean and functional?
  • Are there additional skills that would be helpful for the youth to acquire?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Does housework independently; maintains a clean and functional space.
  • 1 – Maintains a reasonably clean space but struggles with common challenges.
  • 2 – Has challenges with housework; needs prompts or reminders.
  • 3 – Not able to do housework; environment may pose a health risk.

Money Management

This indicator describes youth’s ability to manage finances by budgeting and adjusting expenses.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the youth manage their money?
  • Can they meet their monthly expenses?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Manages money independently; understands budgeting.
  • 1 – Some challenges (e.g., overspending) but no notable impact on functioning.
  • 2 – Challenges interfere with functioning.
  • 3 – Not able to manage money.

Communication Device Use

This indicator refers to youth’s ability to use phones and devices appropriately and safely.

Questions to Consider

  • What communication devices does the youth have access to?
  • Do they protect their personal information?
  • Do they engage in dangerous behavior on their device?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Uses and manages devices appropriately and independently.
  • 1 – Some challenges (e.g., boundary issues, device loss) but no major impact.
  • 2 – Challenges with use or access; judgment issues with device use.
  • 3 – Unable to use devices or engages in dangerous/inappropriate activity.

Housing Safety

This indicator describes whether the youth’s current housing circumstances are safe and accessible.

Questions to Consider

  • What are the youth’s current housing circumstances?
  • Is the housing safe?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No challenges; housing fully supports health, safety, and accessibility.
  • 1 – Minor challenges that do not interfere with functioning or pose risk.
  • 2 – Notable limitations that interfere with functioning.
  • 3 – Housing presents significant risk to health and well-being.
