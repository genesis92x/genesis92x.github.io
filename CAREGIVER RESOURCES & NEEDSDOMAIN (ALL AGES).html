Supervision

This indicator rates the caregiver’s capacity to provide the level of monitoring and discipline needed by the child/youth.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the caregiver feel about their ability to set limits and redirect the child/youth?
  • Does the caregiver need additional support with supervision or monitoring?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No need for action; caregiver provides consistent and effective supervision.
  • 1 – Generally adequate supervision but inconsistent; may need occasional help.
  • 2 – Supervision is inconsistent or frequently absent; assistance needed.
  • 3 – Unable to monitor or discipline; child/youth is at risk due to lack of supervision.

Involvement with Care

This indicator rates the caregiver’s participation in the child/youth’s care and ability to advocate for them.

Questions to Consider

  • How involved is the caregiver in services for the child/youth?
  • Is the caregiver an advocate for the child/youth?
  • Do they participate in planning services?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Fully involved and an effective advocate.
  • 1 – Consistently involved but not an active advocate; open to support.
  • 2 – Not actively involved in services or interventions.
  • 3 – Wishes for child/youth to be removed from care.

Knowledge

This indicator identifies the caregiver’s understanding of the child/youth’s strengths and needs.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the caregiver understand the child/youth’s diagnoses or symptoms?
  • Do their expectations reflect an understanding of the child/youth’s challenges?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Fully knowledgeable about the child/youth’s strengths and needs.
  • 1 – Some mild deficits in understanding.
  • 2 – Significant deficits in understanding or relating to the child/youth.
  • 3 – Little or no understanding; places child/youth at risk.

Organization

This indicator rates the caregiver’s ability to organize and manage their household within the context of services.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the caregiver struggle with appointments or schedules?
  • Do they need help managing tasks?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Well organized and efficient.
  • 1 – Minimal difficulties; may occasionally forget appointments.
  • 2 – Moderate difficulty organizing and maintaining household.
  • 3 – Unable to organize household; needs immediate help.

Social Resources

This indicator rates the social assets and resources that the caregiver can bring to bear in addressing the needs of the child/youth and family.

Questions to Consider

  • Which family members or friends can the caregiver call on for help?
  • Does the caregiver have emotional support?
  • Can the caregiver rely on others to help with caregiving?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Significant social and family networks actively help with caregiving.
  • 1 – Some support from family, friends, or social network.
  • 2 – Needs work to engage support network.
  • 3 – No support network available.

Residential Stability

This indicator rates the housing stability of the caregiver(s).

Questions to Consider

  • Are there concerns about the housing situation?
  • Has the caregiver experienced homelessness or instability?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Stable housing with no known risks.
  • 1 – Relatively stable but with recent moves or potential issues.
  • 2 – Multiple moves in the past year; unstable housing.
  • 3 – Currently homeless or recently experienced homelessness.

Medical/Physical

This indicator refers to medical or physical problems that limit the caregiver’s ability to provide care.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the caregiver have health problems that limit caregiving?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No health problems; caregiver is generally healthy.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of health issues; in recovery.
  • 2 – Health problems interfere with caregiving.
  • 3 – Health problems prevent caregiving.

Mental Health

This indicator refers to serious mental health issues among caregivers that may limit their caregiving capacity.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the caregiver have mental health needs that affect caregiving?
  • Is there evidence of transgenerational trauma?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No mental health difficulties.
  • 1 – History or suspicion of mental health issues; in recovery.
  • 2 – Mental health difficulties interfere with caregiving.
  • 3 – Mental health difficulties prevent caregiving.

Substance Use

This indicator rates the impact of caregiver substance use on their ability to provide care.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the caregiver have substance use needs that affect parenting?
  • Is the caregiver in treatment?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No substance use issues.
  • 1 – History or mild use; in recovery; no interference with caregiving.
  • 2 – Substance use interferes with caregiving.
  • 3 – Substance use prevents caregiving.

Developmental

This indicator describes developmental disabilities that challenge the caregiver’s ability to provide care.

Questions to Consider

  • Does the caregiver have developmental challenges that affect caregiving?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No developmental needs.
  • 1 – Developmental challenges present but not interfering.
  • 2 – Developmental challenges interfere with caregiving.
  • 3 – Developmental challenges prevent caregiving.

Safety

This indicator describes the caregiver’s ability to maintain the child/youth’s safety within the household.

Questions to Consider

  • Is the caregiver able to protect the child/youth from harm?
  • Are there individuals in the home who may be abusive?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Household is safe and secure.
  • 1 – Safe but concerns exist due to history or others in the home.
  • 2 – Child/youth is in some danger from individuals with access to the home.
  • 3 – Child/youth is in immediate danger from individuals with unsupervised access.

Marital/Partner Violence in the Home

This indicator describes conflict in the caregiver’s relationship and its impact on caregiving.

Questions to Consider

  • How do the caregiver and partner resolve conflict?
  • Does conflict escalate to aggression or violence?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No notable conflict; disagreements handled respectfully.
  • 1 – Occasional conflict; generally minimized when child/youth is present.
  • 2 – Frequent arguments; verbal aggression or property destruction witnessed by child/youth.
  • 3 – Escalates to violence; child/youth is at risk.

Caregiver Emotional Responsiveness

This indicator refers to the caregiver’s ability to understand and respond to the child/youth’s emotional needs.

Questions to Consider

  • How does the caregiver respond to challenging behaviors?
  • Are they emotionally responsive when the child/youth is happy or upset?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – Emotionally empathic and responsive.
  • 1 – Generally responsive but occasionally misses emotional cues.
  • 2 – Often not empathic; frequently unable to meet emotional needs.
  • 3 – Rarely or never emotionally responsive; significant difficulties present.

Caregiver Legal

This indicator rates the caregiver’s involvement with the justice system, including immigration issues.

Questions to Consider

  • Has the caregiver been arrested or incarcerated?
  • Are there legal or immigration issues affecting caregiving?

Ratings and Descriptions

  • 0 – No legal difficulties.
  • 1 – History of legal problems; not currently involved with the system.
  • 2 – Currently involved in the legal system.
  • 3 – Serious legal issues; at risk of incarceration; needs immediate intervention.
